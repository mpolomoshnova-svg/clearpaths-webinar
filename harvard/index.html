Most Massachusetts families assume proximity to elite universities gives their child an advantage. It doesn't. In fact, students from Boston-area prep schools face some of the most competitive applicant pools in the country.

What separates the students who get in from those who don't isn't a perfect GPA or a long list of clubs. It's how their entire profile is constructed — and most families don't start thinking about that until it's already too late.

Join this exclusive session to learn:

Inside Ivy League's applicant review: what the admissions office actually prioritizes beyond grades and test scores

Why your child's high school context shapes how their application is read — and how to use that strategically

The difference between activities that fill a résumé and those that signal genuine leadership to Ivy readers

The patterns behind unexpected rejection letters — and the decisions your family can still control right now

Live Q&A with an Ivy League insider — get real answers to your most pressing questions before application season begins
