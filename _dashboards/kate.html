Your Questions Answered
Question 1
"What is the most desirable profile to have when applying to colleges?"

This is one of the most important questions a family can ask. Here's the honest answer:

UC admissions readers are not looking for the "most impressive" student. They're looking for the most interesting, intentional, and authentic one.

The "desirable profile" has 4 dimensions:

1. Academic Foundation

Strong GPA + course rigor that shows you challenged yourself with what was available. Kate has this — 3.6–3.8 with APs demonstrates solid academic commitment.

2. A Clear "Spike"

Not 10 random activities — one area where you go deeper than everyone else. Kate's spike is dance: varsity + competitive = serious dedication. The key is turning this depth into a narrative.

3. Initiative & Leadership

Not just "I participated." Admissions readers want: "I saw a problem and did something about it." A title (president, captain) helps, but what matters more is evidence of action and impact.

4. Authentic PIQ Essays

Four 350-word essays that reveal who Kate is beyond the numbers. UCs use these to understand context, character, and contribution. The best essays come from real experiences, not manufactured ones.

Question 2
"What should I be focusing on during my summer after junior year?"
This summer is the most important summer of Kate's high school career. It's the last chance to build experiences that will appear on the UC application. Here's exactly what we'd prioritize:

☀️ Kate's Summer 2026 Priority Plan

June → August — the last summer before applications open

🔴 Priority 1: Launch a Dance-Based Initiative

This is the single highest-impact action. Start a project that combines Kate's dance expertise with community impact: teach dance workshops for younger kids, create a choreography program for underserved communities through Philanthropy Club, or launch a dance fitness initiative. Even 6–8 weeks of a focused initiative creates a powerful story for PIQ essays. The point isn't scale — it's intentionality and follow-through.

🔴 Priority 2: Begin PIQ Essay Brainstorming

The UC application asks Kate to choose 4 of 8 Personal Insight Questions (350 words each). Starting in summer means she's drafting from real experiences — not scrambling in November. We'd map which prompts align best with Kate's story: Prompt 2 (creativity/dance), Prompt 5 (challenge/growth), Prompt 7 (community/philanthropy), and Prompt 6 (academic interest). Early brainstorming = stronger, more authentic essays.

🟡 Priority 3: Dual Enrollment Course

If summer community college courses are still available, take one. It shows academic initiative beyond high school and adds a "college-level coursework" signal. Choose something aligned with interests — psychology, sociology, or communication would complement Kate's performing arts background.

🟡 Priority 4: Finalize School List + Research Financial Aid

Lock in the final list of 5–7 schools (reach/target/safety) and start researching merit scholarships and financial aid deadlines for each. Some scholarship applications open in fall — knowing them now means no missed opportunities.
