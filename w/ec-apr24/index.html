Most California parents are optimizing for the wrong things

UC uses Comprehensive Review — 13 factors. Activities are one of the loudest signals in the file, and the one where parents burn the most money on the wrong strategy.

Myth What parents chase

  • Quantity over pattern. 12 clubs, 3 sports, 4 summer programs — readers call this a scattergun file.
  • Pay-to-play "leadership" programs that every admissions office has already seen a thousand times.
  • Volunteer-hour totals as a number, with no through-line between them.
  • Captain/president titles added in senior year.
  • "Passion project" websites spun up two months before the application.

What readers weigh

  • Depth + trajectory in 2–3 areas, visible across multiple years.
  • Evidence of initiative: something that wouldn't have existed without your student.
  • Impact on other people, not just participation totals.
  • Context for constraints: work hours, caregiving, commute — readers adjust for this.
  • A coherent story between activities, essays, and intended major.
